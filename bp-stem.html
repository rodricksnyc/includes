What is EEO-1?

What is the EEO-1 Report?

The EEO-1 Component 1 Report is a compliance data collection mandated by Federal Statute and Regulations (Civil Rights Act of 1964, as amended). The data collection requires employers to report the number of employees categorized by race/ethnicity, gender and job category.

How do the EEOC and Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs (OFCCP) use EEO- 1 Component 1 data?

The EEO-1 Component 1 Report data are used by the EEOC and Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs (OFCCP) to analyze employment patterns, such as the representation of women and minorities within companies, industries or regions. These efforts are to support enforcement of the Civil Rights Act of 1964.

Filing deadline

What is the EEO-1 filing deadline?

The filing deadline is typically three months after data collection launches. The 2019 and 2020 EEO-1 Data Collection is expected to launch in April 2021 and close in July 2021. The exact dates will be posted after data collection officially launches, and this FAQ will be updated.

Can filers request an extension?

Extensions will not be granted, except under extenuating circumstances beyond the filer’s control, and with the EEOC’s approval.

Data security and confidentiality

Is EEO-1 data confidential?

Federal statute prohibits EEOC from making public employment data derived from any of its compliance data collections (i.e. Section 709(e) of Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, as amended).

Can the public request employment data for federal contractors?

In select cases, the public may obtain employment data for federal [government] contractors companies that hold the prime contract, or companies that are a first-tier subcontractors to the prime. Any requests for employment data for federal contractors (or first-tier subcontractors must be directed to the Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs (OFCCP) in the U.S. Department of Labor.

How does the EEOC respond to a Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests for an employer’s EEO-1 report?

The EEOC assesses FOIA requests at the time they are received. FOIA Exemptions 3 and 4 may protect an employer’s EEO-1 reports from disclosure. FOIA Exemption 3 covers information that is prohibited from disclosure by another federal law. Section 709(e) of Title VII, in turn, forbids “any [EEOC] officer or employee” from making “public in any manner whatever any information obtained by the Commission . . . prior to the institution of any [Title VII] proceeding . . . involving such information.” FOIA Exemption 4 protects privileged and confidential trade secrets and commercial or financial information. Pursuant to the Supreme Court’s recent decision, Food Marketing Institute v. Argus Leader Media, --- S. Ct. ---, 2019 WL 2570624 (June 24, 2019), Exemption 4 protects information that is customarily and actually treated as private by its owner and provided to the government under an assurance of privacy.

What companies must file?

What companies are required to file the EEO-1 report?

The following companies are required to file the EEO-1 report annually:
  1. Private employers with 100 or more employees.
  2. Federal government prime contractors or first-tier subcontractors subject to Executive Order 11246, as amended with both 50 or more employees and a prime contractor first-tier subcontract amounting to
    $50,000 or more.
  3. Companies that serve as a depository of Government funds in any amount or is a financial institution which is an issuing and paying agent for U.S. Savings Bonds and Savings Notes and have 50 or more employees.

If a company or establishment has less than 100 employees, but is associated with other company(s) or parent company where the entire enterprise employs a total of 100 or more employees. Do we need to file an EEO-1 report?

If an establishment is affiliated through common ownership and/or centralized management with other entities in an enterprise with a total employment of 100 or more, the headquarters or parent company must file the EEO-1 report. The headquarters or parent company must file for all its subsidiaries and establishments/locations.

Does a company need to file an EEO-1 report if it has less than 50 employees but has a federal government contract worth $50,000 or more?

No, a company must meet both requirements of 50 employees and the government contract worth $50,000 or more.

Will companies need to file data for both 2019 and 2020 in April 2021?

Companies must file the EEO-1 report for each year they are in business and meet the filing requirements. In April 2021, eligible companies will be required to first file for 2019, then file for 2020 after the 2019 report is submitted.

Business information

What is the significance of the FEIN identification number?

Filers must identify the employer identification number (FEIN) for each company establishment with a different address and/or NAICS code. The FEIN is assigned by the IRS, and is used to identify business entities. Companies will be required to provide an FEIN for each establishment location when filing the EEO-1 annual report.

What is the appropriate NAICS code?

The North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) codes are standardized classification system that help define the primary business activity of an establishment or company. To identify the correct NAICS code, please reference the useful resources on the NAICS website: https://www.naics.com/what-is-a-naics-code-why-do-i-need-one/

How to count employees

What is a workforce snapshot period?

The workforce snapshot period is the pay period selected to count employees.

Which workforce snapshot period (i.e. time period) must companies use to extract EEO-1 employment data?

Employment data must be pulled from one pay period in October, November or December of the current data collection year. This is referred to as the workforce snapshot period.

If my employment level fluctuates above and below 100 employees in October through December, am I obligated to choose a workforce snapshot period that consists of 100 or more employees?

No. While employers are encouraged to select a period with more than 100 employees, they are not obligated to choose a period with 100 or more employees. The employer may choose any workforce snapshot period between October 1 and December 31 of the reporting year.

Do companies need to select the same workforce snapshot period for 2019 and 2020?

Companies may select different snapshot periods for 2019 and 2020.

Which employees to include in the report

Are employers required to report employees who were employed during the selected workforce snapshot period even if they were no longer active employees by December 31st of the data collection year?

Yes. Even if an employee resigned or was terminated before December 31 of that year, the employee should be reported if the employee was employed during the selected workforce snapshot period.

Should part-time employees be included in the report?

Yes. Both full and part-time employees are included in EEO-1 reporting.

Does the EEO-1 report require data on applicants who were not hired/employed?

No. The EEO-1 Component 1 report requires data on employees only.

How should employees who telework (i.e. work from home) be reported on the EEO-1 report?

Employees who telework, i.e. work from home, must be included in the EEO-1 report for the establishment to which they report. DO NOT include home addresses for these employees as a company location.

How to classify employees into categories

How do employers determine an employee’s race and/or ethnicity?

Employers are required to offer employees an opportunity to self-identify for the purposes of the EEO-1 report. This information can be collected on applications or a voluntary survey. The employer would collect the data voluntarily and aggregate this information into the EEO-1 report. When collecting information, employers should notify employees that providing this information is voluntary.

Can an employer write in an employee's ethnicity if they think they know?

Self-identification by the employee is the preferred method of identifying the race and ethnic information necessary for the EEO-1 report. Employers are required to offer employees the opportunity to use self-identification to complete the EEO-1 report. If an employee declines to self-identify, employment records or observation may be used.

Does the EEO-1 report collect employee non-binary gender data?

No. The EEO-1 report continues to only collect either male or female binary employee gender data. A filer may report employee data for non-binary gender employees on the Remarks page in the online filing system. Please preface the remark with the phrase “Additional Employee Data”.

How do employers classify employees into the job categories listed in the EEO-1 report?

The job categories are based on employee skill level, knowledge and responsibilities and are designed to reflect common company hierarchies. Please select the category that best reflects employees in the company.

To help, there is a job classification guide that maps Standard Occupational Classifications (SOC) and Census Job Codes to the job classifications, which can be accessed here.

Detailed descriptions of the job classifications can also be found in the instructional booklet, which can be accessed here.

For a summary guide of the job classifications, please visit the fact sheet on job categories here.

Foreign (non-U.S.) affiliations

Do companies report establishments or locations that are outside of the U.S.?

Companies should not include establishments or locations that are outside of the 50 United States or the District of Columbia. No reports should be filed for establishments in Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands or other American Protectorates.

How do companies report employees if the company is foreign-owned?

For companies that are foreign-based without a US-based headquarters, each subsidiary or holding within the U.S. may file its own EEO-1 report. In this instance, one subsidiary may take responsibility for filing for all company entities in the U.S., but the companies are not required to file jointly. However, if is a US-based headquarters, the headquarters would be responsible for filing for any and all subsidiaries/holdings in the U.S.

Single or multi-establishment

What is the difference between a single-establishment and a multi-establishment company?

A single-establishment company does business at only one physical address. A multi-establishment company does business at two or more physical addresses.

Single-establishment companies are only required to submit one EEO-1 data record/report. Multi-establishment companies are required to submit a) separate report for the headquarters, b) separate report for each establishment of the company with 50 or more employees, c) separate report for each establishment with fewer than 50 employees, and d) consolidated report that includes all employees.

How do employers report employees who work at client sites?

Employers have the option to either a) report employees working at client sites using the client site address as the location of the establishment, or b) roll those up to a non-client site employer address.

How do I file?

What are the options for submitting the EEO-1 Report?

The preferred and most efficient method of submitting Component 1 EEO-1 reports is through the EEO-1 Online Filing System or as an electronically transmitted datafile (ASCII/TEXT or CSV/XML file) via a data file upload.  The filer support page on the website will include data file upload instructions in April 2021, when data collection launches. Specifications will be similar to previous years. For a summary of the data employers will need to collect, please see the factsheets on Preparing Data – Single-establishment here, or Preparing Data – Multi-establishment here.

Is a company able to file for previous years if it was required but failed to do so before the deadline?

No. There is no way to file for previous years once the data collection has closed. The EEO-1 online filing system does not allow employers to file for previous years. In 2021, filers will file for 2019 and 2020 because the 2019 filing period was suspended due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Companies filing for the first time

If a company has never filed an EEO-1 Report before, how can the filer get started?

Once the registration system is open, navigate to the registration page, and follow the instructions. After submitting the registration form, file the company number and password for that is provided.

Registration is currently not open. Please submit contact information on the contact page here to receive updates when the registration system is open.

If a company is required to file the EEO-1 report for the first time, how does a filer obtain an EEO-1 company number and passcode?

Once the registration system is open, navigate to the registration page, and follow the instructions for companies filing for the first time. After submitting the registration form, a filer will receive a company ID number and passcode. This will be used to create user accounts for a company.

Companies that have filed before

How can a filer access a company's EEO-1 account?

All filers will need to create a new user account(s) in 2021 using the company number and passcode included in the notification letter. This letter will be mailed to filers in April 2021.

Upon successfully linking user and company account, a filer will be able to access the company’s EEO-1 database.

How can filers update a company’s point of contact(s) for the EEO-1 data collection?

Contact the EEO-1 Filer Support Team to submit the company’s updated contact information. This request must be submitted by an authorized official who is currently a point of contact, or include a letter from an authorized official of the company on company letterhead.

How can a filer view a company's prior years' reports?

Company reports from 2015, 2016, 2017, and 2018 will be available when data collection launches in April 2021.

Are companies able to correct employment information provided in prior years' reports?

No. Companies are not able to correct nor update employment information reported in prior years.

Account and log-in information

Can filers use login information from EEO-1 Component 2?

No. The Component 2 account information cannot be used to access the EEO-1 Component Online Filing System. These are two different data collections, using distinct systems.

How can a filer create an account?

When the online filing system opens, each user will create a unique user account. This will be a new account specifically for the 2019 and 2020 EEO-1 Component 1 filing. Once the user account is created, authorized staff can use the company passcode provided in the advance notification letter to link the company to their account. If a user is authorized to file EEO-1 reports for multiple companies, they can link multiple companies to their user profile.

What should a filer do if a company did not receive or cannot locate the advance notification letter that includes the company passcode?

The letters will be mailed in April 2021. If a company does not receive the letter by the end of April, check the website for any applicable updates. If necessary, contact the EEO-1 Filer Support team to obtain the company passcode, and update the contact information if need. It will be necessary to verify that the filer is authorized to receive this information. This will be based on being an existing point of contact in our records, or by providing a letter on company letterhead authorizing filer access.

The company point of contact has changed since the last data collection. How does a company update a point of contact?

Please use the contact us form on www.eeocdata.gov to submit updated contact information. In the request, please send the following information:

  1. Company Name
  2. FEIN
  3. Company ID
  4. Company Passcode
  5. New Contact Name
  6. New Contact Email
  7. New Contact Phone Number
  8. Whether this replaces or add to existing contacts
  9. New Mailing Address (if applicable)

Can companies include more than one point of contact to receive all communications?

Yes. Companies can include more than one person as the point of contact. Companies can also add additional contacts.

Mergers

If a company has experienced a merger since the last data collection period. How should these changes be reflected in this year's report?

Mergers which occurred since the last data collection period should be reported to the EEO-1 Filer Support Team through the filer support page. This can be found here: Support Center. When contacting the support team, please be prepared to provide the following:

  1. Names and addresses of each establishment of the company including the corporate headquarters and subsidiary establishments.
  2. Company ID and/or FEIN for each company (if known).
    3. Name and address of the new company (if applicable). New company contact information including, name, title, email, and phone number.
Acquisitions

If a company has acquired another company since the last data collection period. How should these changes be reflected in this year's report?

Acquisitions which occurred since the last data collection period should be reported to the EEO-1 Filer Support Team through the filer support page. This can be found here: Support Center. When contacting the support team, please be prepared to provide the following:

  1. Names and addresses of each establishment of the company including the corporate headquarters and subsidiary establishments.
  2. Company ID and/or FEIN for each company (if known).
  3. Name and address of the new company (if applicable).
  4. New company contact information including, name, title, email, and phone number.
  5. Specify which company is the acquiring company, and which company is the acquired company (company ID of all will be useful).
  6. If the acquiring company acquired a small company that otherwise would not be required to file the EEO-1 report, or has not previously filed, the acquiring company may add establishments and file for the new employees through the online filing system.

If a company just acquired a small company that was not required to file EEO-1 Reports in the past. Does the company still need to contact the Filer Support Team before filing the EEO-1 report?

When a company requires another company that would otherwise not be required to file EEO-1 Reports, the acquiring company can proceed with adding the acquired small company’s establishment(s) to the acquiring company in the online filing system. There is no need to report this change to EEO-1 Filer Support Team prior to filing an EEO-1 report.

Spinoffs

If a company has experienced a spinoff since the last data collection period. How should these changes be reflected in this year's report?

Spinoffs which occurred since the last data collection period should be reported to the EEO-1 Filer Support Team through the filer support page. This can be found here: Support Center. When contacting the support team, please be prepared to provide the following:

  1. Names and addresses of each establishment of the company including the corporate headquarters and subsidiary establishments.
  2. Company ID and/or FEIN for each company (if known).
  3. Name and address of the new company (if applicable).
  4. New company contact information including, name, title, email, and phone number.
  5. Specify which is the current parent company, and which is the new spun off company.
  6. Specify the new headquarters for the new spun off company, as well as the list of all physical addresses or locations that will serve as establishments.
Bankruptcy

If a company has filed for bankruptcy, are they still required to file the EEO-1 report?

Depending on the effective date of the bankruptcy and the workforce snapshot period selected, the company may or may not be required to submit an EEO-1 report. If the company selects a pay period that occurred prior to the finalization of the bankruptcy, the company is required to file. However, if a company completed bankruptcy prior to the selected workforce snapshot period, then they must contact the EEOC Filer Support Team through the filer support page with documentation showing that the company is now out of business and has ceased operations. The filer support page can be found here: Support Center. The company will then be removed from the mailing list. Until the bankruptcy is complete, the company must continue to file EEO-1 reports as required.

Timing of Business Changes

How does a company handle filing both 2019 and 2020 EEO-1 reports if a merger, acquisition, spinoff, or bankruptcy occurred in the past two years?

For 2019 and 2020, report according to the status of the company during the workforce snapshot period selected for each year. This may be before or after the company change, and it may result in reporting differently for 2019 vs 2020.

Professional Employer Organizations (PEO)

Is a PEO required to report client employees that do not meet the filing threshold?

A PEO is not required to report employees for a client company that would not be required to if they were not in the PEO relationship. For example, a single establishment company and non-federal contractor with 40 employees would not report under the existing rule. Therefore, these co-employees would not be reported by a PEO.

If a PEO does not provide services for all of a client company’s employees, is the PEO or company responsible for filing an EEO-1 report?

When a PEO and a client company’s contracting agreement does not include 100 percent of the covered client company’s employees, the covered client company, not the PEO, is responsible for filing the EEO-1 report.

Which NAICS code(s) should a PEO use for its headquarters and any establishment reports?

PEOs should use NAICS Code 561330 – Professional Employer Organizations, to file for their headquarters and any establishment locations in which the major business activity is in providing human resources and human resource management services to client businesses. All client company establishments should be assigned NAICS based on major business activity at the establishment.

If an organization’s EEO-1 reporting is handled by a PEO, can the EEOC Employer Data Team provide the organization with its establishment EEO-1 reports or any client company data directly?

No. The PEO is considered the W-2 employer of record. As such, the EEOC Employer Data Team may only provide the PEO’s establishment reports and client data to the PEO EEO-1 company contact.

Can a PEO submit 2019 and 2020 EEO-1 reports for one client company before filing 2019 and 2020 EEO-1 reports for another client company?

No. Filers must first file 2019 reports for all of client companies prior to filing EEO-1 reports for 2020.
